Project Progression

A chronological record of the investigation. This file documents the critical pivot from the initial branding experiment to the final crime-based board game, backed by supervision logs and peer critiques.

Status: Active Trail Format: Chronology Use: Track decisions
Phase 01: Initial Direction

The Branding Experiment

The project began by exploring how participatory branding practices expose the ethical implications of marketing the human body. Early frameworks were grounded in Naomi Klein's No Logo and interactive installations.

Phase 02: The Pivot

Shifting the Investigation

The installation route was visually interesting, but it was too passive: the audience could observe branding and bias without having to make a decision. Research into game systems, true-crime structures, and rapid judgement showed that a board game could place players inside the problem. The pivot made the project more active, measurable, and connected to the central question of how people build guilt from limited surface evidence.

Phase 03: Game Mechanics

Prototyping the Board Game

With the new direction locked in, focus shifted to game design, rule mechanics, and integrating the true-crime aesthetic into the physical components.
