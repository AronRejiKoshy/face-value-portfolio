System Overview & Mechanics

Concept Directive

Face Value is a social deduction board game designed to explore how players construct suspicion from limited, brand-coded information. Players are presented with a crime case and a set of suspects. Through interaction, discussion, and evidence collection, they must determine who is responsible. The system is engineered to reveal how narrative and perception override factual reasoning.

Player Role Parameters

Each player assumes the identity of a suspect connected to the victim. Players are not assigned as the murderer. Instead, they participate in the process of investigation, discussion, and accusation. Their role is to interpret evidence, form narratives, and influence collective judgment.

Operational Flow

  1. Setup: Players are assigned character profiles and initial context is given.
  2. Investigation: Players move between locations, collecting evidence and are allowed to use character abilities.
  3. Discussion: Players share interpretations and form suspicions based on available information.
  4. Judgment: Players return to a central location and vote on who they believe is responsible.
  5. Reveal: The true cause of the crime and outcome is revealed.

Core Mechanic Vulnerability

The system is structured to prioritize interpretation over objective truth. Evidence is distributed in a way that requires cross-referencing, yet players are more likely to form conclusions based on character perception and narrative coherence. This creates a space where branding, lifestyle cues, and identity markers influence judgment more strongly than factual evidence.

ARCHIVE NO. 441B

Physical Apparatus

Spatial Layout

  • Location Tiles: 5-6 tiles representing physical spaces (Example: Gym, café, apartment, university space, shopping mall).
  • Central Space: "Court" or Discussion Area (can be adapted based on the specific case parameters).

Voting System

Public Voting: Tokens placed on suspects.
Private Recording Sheet: Questions/prompts to guide the player (utilised as technical depth for data extraction).

Movement System

Each round, the player chooses where they want to go. They can use their "ability" to draw relevant evidence based on the location tile.

Evidence Cards

Object cards. Ideally, these are the branded objects affiliated with the victim, the suspect, or other players. Content cards provide more information about location or timeline hints.

Character Cards

Profiles containing baseline data: Portrait, Name, Occupation, Lifestyle markers, Traits, Flaws, and unique "Abilities". Designed to trigger instant profiling.

ARCHIVE NO. 441D

Protocol: HTML / CSS / JS

Application: Digital Archiving & Interface Development

To effectively present this project, standard PDF formats were insufficient. The investigation required building a bespoke, interactive digital dossier from the ground up, demanding practical execution of core web technologies to simulate a secure, classified terminal.

Structural Logic (HTML)

Engineered semantic document frameworks. Mastered the use of data attributes to link dynamic elements, creating a scalable foundation capable of housing complex evidence galleries and expandable timelines.

Spatial & Visual Hierarchy (CSS)

Executed advanced CSS Grid and Flexbox models for precise layout alignment. Developed custom animations, interactive hover states, overlay layering, and ensured robust responsive breakpoints for cross-device legibility.

System Functionality (JavaScript)

Authored dynamic DOM manipulation scripts. This operational logic enables the tabbed terminal interfaces, decryptable text logs, modal pop-ups, and the custom image-zoom overlay systems utilized throughout the archive.
